# Phase 01.2: Long-Horizon Recovery Bootstrap and Governance - Research

**Researched:** 2026-04-03
**Domain:** restart governance, software archaeology custody, bounded device
execution, and scientific claim control
**Confidence:** HIGH

## Summary

Phase `01.2` should be treated as a control-plane and bounded-execution phase,
not as a substitute for the later baseline and rebuild work.

The current repo already contains most of the raw ingredients required by the
long-horizon brief:

1. a canonical keep-set for the hybrid archaeology lane
2. a restart PRD, battery schedule, branch register, hardware baseline, and
   lane-specific runbooks
3. a reconciled authority pack for the late hybrid line
4. fresh governed Genesis / SoC ledgers showing that the phone crossed
   executability

What is missing is not a brand-new theory of the project.
What is missing is a single place where the long-horizon mandate is turned into:

- a clean custody surface
- a live state snapshot
- a lane policy tied to current evidence
- explicit gate verdicts
- a bounded blocker memo
- a fresh device probe performed under the current control surface

## Key Scientific Read

- The older governed Genesis / SoC lane remains the strongest authority
  surface.
- The late hybrid line is real, but currently smoke-only when freshly called.
- Two on-device hybrid runner generations still survive.
- Fresh callable hybrid runs still collapse to `t1_contraction` after timestamp
  normalization.
- The narrowest blocker is still binary-to-asset pairing plus missing
  substantive task continuity, not device reachability.

## Research Constraints

- Do not reopen the old question of whether the phone exists as an execution
  target; that is already settled.
- Do not widen GPU claims beyond a receiptable engineering lane.
- Do not use NPU-adjacent hardware presence as runtime proof.
- Do not collapse long-horizon planning into a prose-only governance exercise;
  a fresh bounded run is required in this phase.
