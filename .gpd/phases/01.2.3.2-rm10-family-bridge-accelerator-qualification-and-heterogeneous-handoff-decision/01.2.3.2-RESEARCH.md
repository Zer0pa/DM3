## Question

What is the smallest honest next branch phase after `01.2.3.1` that advances the RM10-primary hypothesis without inflating the existing `F1` and `F2` split into a false heterogeneous success?

## Current Evidence

- `F1` is a governed RM10 CPU control family on `/data/local/tmp/genesis_cli` launched from `/data/local/tmp/SoC_runtime/workspace`.
- `F1` currently reproduces `verify.json=f992e9c833f15d579224c463fd730d6b238cf0e7cab26dc551eef4b01bc124a1` and `solve_h2.json=a33c5cc4a91d1c5bab4adff29d3b56b6cb059f3b2268cc92ea5bf2b201d13364` under one fresh control run.

- No accelerator path yet preserves `F1`.
- No receiptable NPU user-space assist role has been established.
- No explicit pre-handoff and post-handoff artifact exists for a heterogeneous role-partition claim.

## Why A Bridge Phase Is Honest

The branch no longer needs another governance bootstrap. It needs one bounded phase that answers whether the only live accelerator-relevant evidence family speaks for the governed control family or must remain fenced off as residue-only feasibility. This is the smallest next move that sharpens the branch hypothesis rather than widening it.

## Research Conclusions

1. The anchor must remain `F1`, not `F2`.
2. The first decisive evidence needed is repeatability plus identity discipline on `F2`, because a single bounded CPU/GPU pair is still thin.
3. The bridge question is not rhetorical. It must be answered by checking whether the `F1` executable surface exposes any callable accelerator-bearing variant at all.
4. NPU work should remain one bounded assist-feasibility probe unless a user-space command or wrapper is named before execution.
5. Explicit heterogeneous `PASS` remains out of scope unless a same-family handoff artifact appears.
