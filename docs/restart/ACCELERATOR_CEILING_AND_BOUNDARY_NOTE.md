## What Counts As Residue Classification

Residue classification is limited to claims inside the exact `F2` family:

- the exact residue binary and exact harmonic observable family stay fixed
- CPU and GPU-backed rows remain comparable on the same receipt schema
- drift is localized as startup-sensitive, telemetry-correlated, persistent, or
  uninterpretable
- any conclusion updates only the descriptive boundary of `F2`

Residue classification can answer:

- whether the outlier appears one-off or persistent within `F2`
- whether the current `feasibility_only` ceiling remains honest
- whether future residue work should stop because the family is too unstable

## What Does Not Count As Bridge Progress

None of the following is bridge progress:

- improved `F2` stability on the residue binary
- GPU-backed receipt parity inside `F2`
- visibility of GPU, QNN, DSP, or RPC infrastructure on RM10
- any mixed-execution interpretation that lacks explicit handoff artifacts
- any statement that `F2` says what a current `F1` accelerator surface would do

Bridge progress would require new same-family evidence on `F1`, not a better
story about `F2`.

## Hard Boundary Rules

- `F1` remains the governed scientific anchor regardless of `F2` outcome

- negative or abstain outcomes are acceptable; inflated bridge rhetoric is not
- no document may use `F2` to imply a same-family accelerator-bearing `F1`
  variant
- no document may use opaque accelerator activity as explicit heterogeneous
